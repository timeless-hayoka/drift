| 1.1 | **Name Availability Search** | Search Delaware Division of Corporations database + USPTO TESS trademark search. ## 2. Step-by-Step Delaware C-Corp Formation

### Option A: Stripe Atlas (Recommended for Solo Founders / Speed)

Stripe Atlas is the fastest, most cost-effective route for a clean, standard Delaware C-Corp.

| Step | Action | Cost |
|------|--------|------|
| 2A.1 | Apply at stripe.com/atlas. Pay one-time fee. | $500 |
| 2A.2 | Complete company details (name, founder info, business description). | Included |
| 2A.3 | Stripe files Certificate of Incorporation with Delaware. | Included |
| 2A.4 | Receive filed COI, EIN from IRS, and post-incorporation documents. | Included |
| 2A.5 | Sign documents via Stripe Dashboard (Bylaws, Initial Board Consent, Stock Purchase Agreements). | Included |
| 2A.6 | Stripe opens a Mercury bank account (optional, recommended). | Free |
| 2A.7 | Access Stripe-issued standard templates (founder stock, IP assignment, bylaws). | Included |
| | **Total First-Year Cost** | **~$500 + DE Franchise Tax (~$400 min) + RA (~$100)** |

**Pros:** Fast (1–2 weeks), includes EIN, clean templates, free Mercury account, trusted by VCs.  
**Cons:** Less customizable than a lawyer; template documents only.

### Option B: Clerky (Recommended for Multi-Founder with Vesting Complexity)

Clerky offers more granular control over founder agreements and is lawyer-friendly.

| Step | Action | Cost |
|------|--------|------|
| 2B.1 | Create account at clerky.com. | Free |
| 2B.2 | Complete company formation questionnaire. | $99–$399 (depending on package) |
| 2B.3 | Clerky generates and files DE Certificate of Incorporation. | Included in package |
| 2B.4 | Generate Bylaws, Board Consent, Stock Purchase Agreements, 83(b) templates. | Included |
| 2B.5 | Founder signs all documents via Clerky. | Included |
| 2B.6 | Apply for EIN separately (Clerky provides guidance) or use their filing service. | Free (DIY) or $100 |
| 2B.7 | Set up bank account separately. | — |
| | **Total First-Year Cost** | **~$200–$600 + DE Franchise Tax + RA** |

**Pros:** More customizable than Atlas, excellent document quality, great for multi-founder vesting setups.  
**Cons:** Slightly more manual than Atlas; EIN and banking not fully automated.

### Option C: Startup Lawyer (Recommended if Complex IP or Non-Standard Terms)

Engage a startup-focused law firm (e.g., Gunderson Dettmer, Cooley, Wilson Sonsini, or a reputable boutique).

| Step | Action | Cost |
|------|--------|------|
| 2C.1 | Engage law firm; sign engagement letter. | — |
| 2C.2 | Lawyer conducts name clearance and drafts custom COI. | $3,000–$8,000 |
| 2C.3 | Lawyer files with Delaware and obtains EIN. | Included |
| 2C.4 | Lawyer drafts custom founder agreements, bylaws, IP assignment. | Included in package or $2,000–$5,000 |
| 2C.5 | Founder review and execution. | — |
| 2C.6 | Lawyer advises on cap table, vesting, and fundraising readiness. | Ongoing |
| | **Total First-Year Cost** | **~$5,000–$15,000 + DE Franchise Tax + RA** |

**Pros:** Fully custom, personal legal advice, best for complex IP (e.g., university tech transfer, prior employer IP risk), non-standard terms.  
**Cons:** Expensive, slower (2–6 weeks), overkill for a clean solo-founder software startup.

## 3. Post-Incorporation Tasks (Critical — Do Within 30 Days)

These steps are legally and financially critical. Missing them can create tax or equity problems.

| # | Task | Deadline | Cost | Why It Matters |
|---|------|----------|------|----------------|
| 3.1 | **File 83(b) Election** | **Within 30 days of stock purchase** | Free (mailing cost only) | Elects to pay tax on stock at current (near-zero) value. Without this, you pay tax on gains at vesting — potentially catastrophic. |
| 3.2 | **Open Business Bank Account** | ASAP after receiving EIN | Free | Commingles funds without one; required for investor diligence. |
| 3.3 | **Set Up Cap Table** | ASAP | Free (Google Sheets / Carta free tier) | Tracks ownership. VCs will demand a clean cap table. |
| 3.4 | **Execute IP Assignment Agreements** | ASAP | Free | Transfers all pre-incorporation IP (code, designs, concepts) from founder(s) to the company. |
| 3.5 | **Set Up Accounting / Books** | First month | Free–$50/mo (Wave, Bench, Pilot) | Clean financials are required for fundraising and tax compliance. |
| 3.6 | **Register Foreign Qualification** | If doing business outside DE | $100–$500/state | If DRIFT has offices, employees, or significant nexus in another state. |
| 3.7 | **Obtain Business Licenses** | Before operating | Varies by city/state | Check local requirements. |
| 3.8 | **Set Up Payroll (if applicable)** | Before first hire | $35–$50/mo + per-employee (Gusto, Rippling, Deel) | Required for W-2 employees. |
| 3.9 | **File Delaware Annual Franchise Tax** | March 1 each year | ~$400 minimum (authorized shares method) | Penalties for late filing. |
| 3.10 | **Set Up Company Record Folder** | ASAP | Free | Google Drive folder + physical binder with all formation docs, signed agreements, and Board consents. |

### 83(b) Election — Step by Step

1. Fill out IRS Form 83(b): https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f83b.pdf
2. Include: founder name, address, EIN, stock description, date purchased, fair market value (usually par value ~$0.00001/share), amount paid.
3. Sign and date.
4. Mail **via certified mail, return receipt requested** to the IRS address for your state.
5. Keep a copy for your records, provide a copy to the company, and keep proof of mailing.

> ⚠️ **This is the most commonly missed and most expensive mistake founders make. If you miss the 30-day window, it is irrevocable.**

## 4. Founder Agreements

DRIFT is currently a solo founder (Julien James), but these agreements are still essential for IP protection and future co-founder/investor readiness.

### 4.1 Stock Purchase Agreement (SPA)

- Founder purchases founder stock from the company.
- Sets purchase price (usually par value), number of shares, and representations.
- Includes vesting schedule (standard: 4 years with a 1-year cliff).

**Standard Vesting Terms:**
- 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- 25% vests after 12 months; remainder vests monthly thereafter
- "Single-trigger" acceleration on change of control (optional; discuss with future counsel)
- "Double-trigger" acceleration (change of control + termination) is more investor-friendly

### 4.2 Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement (CIIAA)

- Founder assigns all IP created before and during employment to the company.
- Includes confidentiality obligations.
- **Critical for fundraising:** Investors will verify all IP is owned by the company, not the individual.

### 4.3 Restricted Stock Agreement (if applicable)

- Governs unvested shares.
- Company has right of first refusal (ROFR) and repurchase right on unvested shares.

### 4.4 Future: Co-Founder Agreement

If adding a co-founder later:
- Execute a new SPA with vesting starting from their join date.
- Ensure CIIAA is signed *before* any work begins.
- Consider a Founder Collaboration Agreement outlining roles, decision-making, and separation terms.

### 4.5 Non-Compete / Non-Solicit

- Delaware generally does not enforce non-competes for employees, but CIIAA should include non-solicit and confidentiality.
- Non-compete may be enforceable in sale-of-business context.

## 5. Pre-Seed Preparation

Prepare these items *before* speaking to investors. Clean corporate hygiene accelerates fundraising.

### 5.1 SAFE Notes

Most pre-seed/seed rounds in the U.S. use Y Combinator's Simple Agreement for Future Equity (SAFE).

| Document | Purpose | Template |
|----------|---------|----------|
| SAFE (Valuation Cap, no Discount) | Standard pre-seed instrument | https://www.ycombinator.com/documents |
| SAFE (MFN, no cap) | For very early investors | YC template |
| Side Letter | Custom terms for lead investors | Lawyer-drafted |

**Key Terms to Understand:**
- **Valuation Cap:** Ceiling on the price at which SAFE converts to equity.
- **Discount:** Percentage discount to the next round price (less common now).
- **MFN (Most Favored Nation):** Allows early investor to adopt better terms from later SAFEs.
- **Pro Rata Rights:** Right to invest in future rounds to maintain ownership.

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Download YC SAFE templates
- [ ] Decide target valuation cap (pre-seed AI middleware typically $5M–$15M)
- [ ] Have a lawyer review first SAFE before sending to investors (even if using standard template)
- [ ] Set up DocSend or Google Drive with investor materials

### 5.2 Pitch Deck

Standard 10–12 slide deck for pre-seed:

1. **Problem** — What pain does DRIFT solve?
2. **Solution** — AI cognitive middleware value proposition
3. **Market Size** — TAM/SAM/SOM
4. **Product** — Demo, architecture, or screenshots
5. **Traction** — Users, pilots, waitlist, LOIs
6. **Business Model** — Revenue model, pricing
7. **Competition** — Competitive landscape and moat
8. **Team** — Julien James background and key hires
9. **Financials** — 3-year projection (even if rough)
10. **Ask** — Amount raising, use of funds, timeline
11. **Appendix** — Technical deep dive, customer quotes
